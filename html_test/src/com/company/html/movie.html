History of Film Development

  • Silent Era (late 19th century to 1920s): Early films were silent and relied on visuals and intertitles to tell stories.
  • Sound Films (1920s to 1940s): Sound was introduced, enhancing the cinematic experience with dialogue, sound effects, and music.
  • Golden Age and International Cinema (1950s to 1970s): This period saw the rise of diverse filmmaking styles and themes from around the world.
  • New Wave and Experimental Films (1960s to 1980s): Filmmakers explored new approaches to storytelling and experimented with film techniques.
  • Modern Film and Digital Technology (1990s to present): Digital technology revolutionized filmmaking, enabling new creative possibilities.

My two favorite movies

"Green Book" "Titanic"
"Green Book" is a 2018 American drama film directed by Peter Farrelly. Based on a true story, it follows the journey of a black pianist and his white driver in the 1960s Southern United States. The film explores their unlikely friendship as they navigate racial tensions and discrimination. With the help of the "Green Book," a travel guide for African-American travelers, they find safe places and confront various challenges. "Green Book" is a heartwarming tale of friendship, acceptance, and overcoming prejudice, garnering critical acclaim for its performances and thoughtful storytelling. "Titanic" is a 1997 epic romance-disaster film directed by James Cameron. Set aboard the RMS Titanic, it tells the story of a young aristocrat, Rose, and a working-class artist, Jack, who fall in love amidst the ship's ill-fated maiden voyage. The film captures their passionate romance against the backdrop of the ship's tragic collision with an iceberg. "Titanic" is known for its breathtaking visuals, immersive storytelling, and iconic soundtrack. It remains one of the most beloved and successful films of all time, touching hearts with its sweeping love story and historical tragedy.
